## What happens in the classroom

The reading is assigned the night before. In class, the teacher opens with a quick check on the two reflection questions and uses the third case study (the ice cream / drowning one) as a launchpad for the day's lesson on confounders. There is no required in-class activity tied to the reading itself.

## Learning goals in detail

Students should leave able to spot the most common confounder pattern: a third variable that drives both the supposed cause and the supposed effect. The reading deliberately uses one case where the confounder is clear and one where it is genuinely contested in the literature.

## Notes for instructors

The third case study divides students reliably — half conclude there's a confounder and half don't. This disagreement is the whole point. If everyone agrees on the third case, push them: "what would you need to measure to be sure?" Look for students who can name a specific variable.
