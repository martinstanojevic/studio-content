## What happens in the classroom

Day 1 is an in-class kickoff: students pick a question and draft a protocol with teacher feedback. The middle of the project is asynchronous — students collect data on their own time over two weeks. The final session is a poster fair where each student explains their question, method, and findings to a rotating group.

## Learning goals in detail

The hardest skill in the project is translating "I want to know if students sleep less during exam week" into something actually measurable with a defensible sample. Students should leave able to defend, on their poster, why their protocol gives evidence relevant to their question — and where it falls short.

## Notes for instructors

Approve every protocol before students leave Day 1, even if it means staying late. Bad protocols (sample size of 3, leading questions, etc.) are nearly impossible to fix mid-project. Strong evidence of understanding shows on the poster's "limitations" section — students who write a thoughtful one usually understood the project.
