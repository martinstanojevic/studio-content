## What happens in the classroom

Students pair up, each pair gets a die and a tally sheet, and they roll 50 times. Pairs report their counts at the front of the room and the teacher pools the class data on the board. Students compare their individual tally to the pooled tally to the theoretical 1/6, 1/6, ... distribution.

## Learning goals in detail

The arc moves from "my pair's results look weird" to "my class's results look pretty close to uniform" — making the law of large numbers tangible without naming it yet. Students should leave able to explain why a single pair's tally is not strong evidence the die is biased.

## Notes for instructors

Watch for the pair that rolls a 6 ten times and concludes the die is loaded — this is the teachable moment. If you have time, repeat the pooling at n=100 per pair to drive the lesson home. Strong evidence of understanding: students predict the pooled distribution will look smoother before you reveal it.
