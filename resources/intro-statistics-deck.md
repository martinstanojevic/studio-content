## What happens in the classroom

The deck is intended as the first lecture of the course. The teacher walks students through two motivating examples (a public-health study and an A/B test), pausing for short polling questions to surface intuitions. The session ends with the framing question that recurs all term: "what is varying, and what model explains the variation?"

## Learning goals in detail

Students should leave with a working sense that statistics is not arithmetic with data — it's a way of reasoning under uncertainty. They should be able to explain in their own words why two samples from the same population can look different.

## Notes for instructors

Don't rush the polling. The point of slides 7 and 12 is to create a moment of disagreement between students before the resolution is shown. If everyone gets it on the first try, the example wasn't doing its job — pick a different motivating dataset for next time.
